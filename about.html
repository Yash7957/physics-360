I've made this site to help anyone who's trying to start learning this amazing (and maybe just a little difficult) subject. When I first started out, I had trouble understanding just exactly what was happening. But I stuck on, studied harder, looked stuff up here and there and found that one golden site with that one sentence, that one analogy, that clarified things a lot more. I've tried to explain, through my eyes, those of not a professional but a learner like everyone else, how I understood the little details and concepts. The site is a huge undertaking, especially if I really cover all the basics. But I'll try nonetheless. I'm Yash Sah. I hope you enjoy!

Start with something here:

Mechanics

We take a look into how things move, why they move and discuss terms such as velocity, displacement and many more. From there, we move to the study of dynamics, where we take a look at what forces are and learn terms such as momentum and impulse. We learn about different kinds of energies and what 'work' in physics is.


Thermodynamics

I try to explain the weirdest concept in perhaps all of physics - entropy, through my perspective. Though I really don't know much about it, I try to share and elucidate what I know. I talk about things such as heat, temperature and the laws of thermodynamics which govern the universe. It's a really interesting topic in physics.


Electrostatics

Charges, batteries, circuits and electrical and electronic appliances. Electrostatics is all about static or 'standing' charges. What are charges? Charges are fundamental entities of atoms - like mass. Why 'standing' charges? Charges create magnetic fields when they move. Physics basically changes a bit and we deal then with 'Electromagnetism'.
